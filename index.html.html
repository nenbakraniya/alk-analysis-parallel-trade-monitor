The Proposition: A Parallel Trade Risk Scoring Model for ALK's European Supply Integrity
As ALK executes Allergy+ and completes paediatric approvals across all five major respiratory allergy tablets in 17+ European countries, each market imports and exports at different volumes under the supply integrity policy. Price differentials between low-price exporting markets (Eastern/Southern Europe) and high-price importing markets (Nordic, Germany, Netherlands) create predictable arbitrage incentives. This framework proposes a structured risk scoring model that could be operationalised as part of the Business Analyst function — enabling proactive monitoring rather than reactive compliance.
Dimension 1 — Price Arbitrage Index
Relative price differential between each country and the EU median. Countries with 30%+ below median are exporter risk; 20%+ above median are importer risk.
Dimension 2 — Parallel Trade Penetration
Historical parallel import market share (Germany 8.5%, Denmark 25%+ in peak periods) used as a structural risk multiplier in the composite score.
Dimension 3 — Regulatory Permissiveness
Pharmacy margin regulation and national licensing activity. Countries with unregulated retail margins show higher parallel trade penetration per academic research.

Tools & Automation
The composite risk score (0–100) is calculated as a weighted average of three dimensions:

Risk Score = (Price Arbitrage Index × 0.45) + (PT Penetration Rate × 0.35) + (Regulatory Permissiveness × 0.20)

Where Price Arbitrage Index = |Country Price – EU Median| / EU Median × 100, capped at 100.
PT Penetration Rate is sourced from academic literature (Dubois & Saethre 2020; Kanavos et al. 2004) and national health authority reports.
Regulatory Permissiveness scores pharmacy margin regulation aggressiveness per country.
Score 0–30 · Low Risk
Monitor quarterly. Standard compliance checks. No supply volume restriction required.
Score 31–60 · Medium Risk
Monitor monthly. Flag variance in ex-factory volumes against in-market demand. Review import licence activity.
Score 61–100 · High / Critical
Weekly monitoring. Align corrections between importing and exporting markets. Escalate to supply integrity policy review if variance exceeds threshold.
A structured weekly workflow for the Business Analyst, directly mapped to the role responsibilities:
Step 1 — Data Collection (Monday)
Pull ex-factory sales by market from ERP. Pull in-market demand data. Identify volume variance per country pair. Flag any anomalies against prior week baseline.
Step 2 — Risk Scoring (Tuesday)
Update composite scores for high-risk markets. Calculate arbitrage index from current price differential data. Run compliance check against supply integrity policy thresholds.
Step 3 — Reporting (Wednesday)
Prepare management reporting with variance commentary. Highlight markets requiring correction alignment. Distribute to regional finance and supply chain stakeholders.
Proposed tooling stack using skills directly evidenced in my application profile:
Data Layer — SQL + Python
Automated ingestion of ex-factory and in-market sales data from ERP/SAP. Python scripts for scoring model calculation and variance detection. Scheduled to run weekly without manual intervention.
Reporting Layer — Power BI / Excel
Interactive dashboard (similar to this prototype) tracking risk scores, volume variances and compliance status by market. Management reporting templates in Excel with variance commentary auto-populated.
Automation Layer — N8N + LLM
Automated alert workflow: when a market crosses a risk threshold, a notification with pre-drafted commentary is generated and routed to the relevant stakeholder. Reduces analyst turnaround time on routine escalations.
